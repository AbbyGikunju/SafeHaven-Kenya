Support Organization and Legal aid

UN Women Kenya

UN Women Kenya collaborates with government agencies, civil society organizations, and other stakeholders to implement programs that focus on ending gender-based violence, promoting women's leadership and political participation, enhancing women's economic empowerment, and ensuring gender-responsive humanitarian action.

The National Gender and Equality Commission

The National Gender and Equality Commission (NGEC) is established by the National Gender and Equality Commission Act. We focus on Special Interest Groups, which include women, youth, persons with disabilities (PWDs), children, the older members of society, minorities and marginalized groups.

Gender Violence Recovery Centre

GVRC is a center of excellence in managing gender-based violence issues in Kenya. It provides free medical treatment and psychosocial support to survivors of GBV.

Coalition on Violence Against Women

COVAW is a national non-profit women's rights organization established to address the silence in Kenyan society regarding violence against women and girls. They work towards eliminating all forms of violence against women.

Usikimye

Usikimye is a trauma-informed and survivor-centered organization in Kenya dedicated to ending the prevalence of sexual and gender-based violence (SGBV). The name "Usikimye" translates to "Do not be silent" in Swahili, reflecting their mission to give survivors a voice and provide refuge.

Healthcare Assistance Kenya

Healthcare Assistance Kenya (HAK) is a humanitarian Non-Governmental Organization that operates the only and the first ever Sexual and Gender Based Violence Rapid Response System and Helpline 1195 that today supports survivors Gender Based Violence (GBV)

National Legal Aid Service

NLAS is a state agency established under the Legal Aid Act 2016, mandated to coordinate the provision of legal aid services to indigent, marginalized, and vulnerable persons in Kenya.




Law Society of Kenya

The Law Society of Kenya offers free legal aid through its Public Interest and Litigation department, focusing on strategic public interest, legislation, access to justice, and protection of public rights.



Federation of Women Lawyers in Kenya

FIDA-Kenya is a non-governmental organization that provides free legal aid to women and advocates for women's rights. They have been instrumental in offering legal assistance to survivors of gender-based violence and have played a significant role in legislative advocacy

Frequently Asked Questions

About Femicide

Femicide is the intentional killing of women and girls because of their gender. It is often perpetrated by intimate partners or family members and is a severe form of gender-based violence.

Femicide is a global issue because it affects women and girls in every country, regardless of culture, religion, or socioeconomic status. It reflects deep-rooted gender inequalities and systemic violence against women.

The main causes include gender inequality, patriarchal norms, lack of legal protection, and societal tolerance of violence against women.
